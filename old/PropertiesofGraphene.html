Further Reading

Graphene is an extremely strong material, having a tensile strength exceeding 100 times that of steel.

For example, a 1 square meter hammock made of graphene could support a 4 kg cat, but only weigh as much as one of the cat's whiskers, at 0.77 mg.

(the same quantity of paper would weigh about a thousand times as much.)

Graphene has excellent thermal and electricial conductivity, with its electrical conductivity being attributed mainly to its structure.

Graphene's hexagonal carbon lattice gives electrons an easy path to traverse and gives graphene the highest electrical conductivity of any known material (at room temperature).

Since graphene is composed entirely of carbon, it it fully biodegradable.

However, research has found that graphene oxide nanoparticles may be toxic to humans!

They can easily contaminate drinking water if they come into contact with it. With continued research, scientists have found that the particles become more unstable and tend to break apart in groundwater, whereas they are mores stable in surface water.
