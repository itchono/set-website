What is Graphene?

Graphene is an allotrope, or structural configuration of carbon.

Graphene's structure is comprised of a hexagonal honeycomb lattice of carbon atoms, where one carbon atom forms each vertex. Graphene is only one atom thick, being almost two-dimensional.

Graphene is like graphite (the stuff in a pencil), but is comprised of only one layer of carbon atoms, rather than a large 3 dimensional block.

(look at the background of this page to see what graphene's structure looks like.)

Graphene is the strongest material to have ever been tested as well as the greatest conductor of heat and electricity ever (at room temperature).

This website contains information about graphene's past, its properties and applications, as well as how graphene will change our future.
